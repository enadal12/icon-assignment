Covid-19 and East Flatbush's Housing Crisis

By: Emily Nadal

East Flatbush, Brooklyn already faced a housing crisis before the pandemic hit. The nieghborhood has become hot spot for development leaving many long time residents priced out of the area. With many out of work or struggling with finances due to COVID-19, housing has become a crisis situation.

Compared to the rest of the city, East Flatbush has a high number of homeowners. Roughly 36.8% of the nieghborhood are homowners, compared to 32.8% citywide.

In 2018, East Flatbush had 380 Foreclosure filings. In 2019, the neighborhood had 213 filings. That number is expected to be higher in 2020 due to the pandemic.

According to ANHD, East Flatbush has a 95.9 % affordable housing risk. The effect of COVID-19 on the area has significantly increased the housing crisis.

East Flatbush had a high rate of COVID-19 cases and deaths. On average the neighborhood had 27.4 cases for every 1,000 residents and 2.7 deaths for every 1,000 residents.

The average rent for an apartment in East Flatbush was $1,350 in 2018.

The average income in East Flatbush in 2018 was $59,050 . The poverty rate was 10.2%.

Sources: NYU Furman Center & Association for Neighborhood and Housing Development
